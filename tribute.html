Aikido

The art of fighting without fighting

Main Characteristics of Aikido

  • It is a defensive-minded martial art
  • Aikido has been developed by Ueshiba Morihei in Japan from 1940s to 1960s
  • During training we handle agressive actions by not hurting the attacker
  • Aikido builds on circular movement and the defending person uses the energy of the attacker
  • In Aikido there aren't any competitions
  • The aim is to improve yourself and help to improve others during training
  • There are grades in Aikido: from 8-1 kyu, then from 1-10 dan
  • You either have white or black belt but some schools use other colours as well
  • Aikido is called sometimes the Art of Peace or Art of Harmony
  • The goal is to be able to manage multiple attackers and divert their agressive energy
  • This can be done through throws, locks and clamps
  • The real goal is to be healthy, have a peace in your life and maintain good physical condition
  • Aikido trainings should be fun, not just struggling. The key is continuous practice
  • We use wooden weapons as well: sword, stick and knife

Impacts of Aikido on the practitioner

  • Overall physical condition is improving
  • The aikidoka learns to handle conflicts cooperatively over time
  • Self-confidence is improving as we handle agressive situations during trainings
  • Physical (and mental) flexibility is increasing
  • Response time is improving
  • Every single part of the body is included in the movements
  • Leg and hip muscles are especially improving
  • Waist and knee pains can be avoided
  • Core muscles are being strenghtened continuously
  • The practitioner will be more active and proactive overall in life
